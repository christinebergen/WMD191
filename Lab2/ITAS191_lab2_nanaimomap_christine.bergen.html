Details about Places:

Descanso Bay Regional Park

Gabriola Island, just a short 20 minute ferry ride from Nanaimo harbour is home to one of my favourite spots: Descanso Bay Regional Park. Descanso Bay Regional Park is situated just slightly north of the ferry terminal on Gabriola Island. Descanso Bay offers a year-round use campground, as well as a day use area that is popular for kayaking, paddle boarding, swimming, and beach combing on the sandstone shores. Various types of whales are often sighted passing by this location.

Ammonite Falls

Ammonite falls is located on the north west side of Mount Benson, and is an impressive waterfall. There are various hiking trails that lead to Ammonite falls, but by far my favourite is the access off of Galloway Gulch. Round trip to the falls and back takes a little over an hour and is a great spot to take your dog off-leash. As always on Vancouver Island, extra care should be taken to be bear and cougar aware, as this is definitely their territory.

Neck Point Park

Neck point is a city park located along Hammond Bay road. Neck point offers great hiking trails, and beautiful coastal and mountanin views. During the spring Herring Spawning every March, Neck Point is a great spot to watch the herring fishery boats and all of the sea lions and sea birds who also show up for a tasty treat.

Crow and Gate Pub

The Crow and Gate Pub is located in an area of south Nanaimo known as Yellow Point. The Crow and Gate has long been a fixture of the food culture in Nanaimo, bringing an authentic British Pub experience to the West Coast. Their pub fare is not unlike what you would find in a traditional pub in England, and they always have a great selection of UK beers on tap. By far the best thing about The Crow and Gate pub, is the Bread Pudding dessert which is only available in the winter months. The Crow and Gate also has an impressive garden/patio area that is open in the summer months.

Drip Coffee

Drip Coffee located across from Departure Bay Beach has become one of my favourite spots to unwind. With the breezy sea air on their patio, it's the perfect spot to meet a friend for coffee or a drink. Their avocado toast is second to none, and they also serve Island Farms ice cream for those hot summer days. Grab a coffee and head across the street to the beach and you can enjoy watching ferries coming and going and float planes taking off and landing.
