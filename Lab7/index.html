Travel

I am passionate about travel and love exploring new places. I have visited 14 different countries (15 if you count Vatican City as it's own country) and can't wait to add more to that list. My favourites include Scotland, Finland, and Estonia. Fun fact: I have visited the most remote inhabited island in the UK, and one of my great-great-grandfathers was actually born there!

Music

Listening to mostly 90's pop and alternative music for most of my life, my musical taste has rapidly evolved in the past few years. This is partly thanks to my Husband, Aaron, who has introduced me to the realm of Canadian Folk music (particularly Stan Rogers), Celtic Punk, and the much more ear-pleasing traditional Irish music known as "Irish Trad". I was inspired by my Husband's ability to play traditional irish music on various instruments and with thanks to my background learning piano as a child and playing in highschool band, have been working on learning to play the Anglo Concertina. Playing the concertina in a traditional music session in Ireland is defintiely on my bucket list.

Board Games

I have found learning new board games to be a fun hobby. Recently we played a collaborative campaign-style game with some friends. It was a unique experience playing a game that requires players to work together towards a common goal rather than competing against each other. The campaign-style was also very interesting, as this particular game was intended to be played over many hours and multiple sessions. After each session, all of the cards and game pieces were packed up into the box so that we were able to easily pick up where we left off when we were able to get together for another session to play.

I never tought I'd be a cat person...

But I'm a cat person. Don't get me wrong, I love a good rent-a-dog situation where I can borrow a dog and take it out for some fun hikes, and then return it to it's owners. Our current cat was a rescue from CatNap Society in Nanaimo. She was a young mom who had a litter of kittens, and had been surrendered to CatNap when her owner could no longer care for her and her kittens. Her kittens were all adopted before she was, and I first went to meet her after her picture and story were shared on the CatNap society facebook page. She had been available for adoption for about 3 months, and had actually been adopted and returned once as the family that adopted her quickly learned she wasn't in favour of being in a house with small children. She is a very outgoing, quirky, affectionate, with a spicy personality and she fits into our household fabulously. She makes sure to give our visitors a very warm welcome and may even swat at her favourites!
